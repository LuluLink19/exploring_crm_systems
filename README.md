## Executive Summary

This project explored Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems through AI-assisted research and evaluation. Research included CRM concepts, product comparisons, open-source CRM exploration, architecture planning, and reflection on AI-assisted workflows. The project demonstrates how AI tools can support software research while still requiring validation and critical analysis.

---

## AI Tools Used

- ChatGPT  
- Vendor documentation  
- Product documentation  
- Online demo environments  
- Additional research sources for validation  

---

## CRM Research Findings

CRM systems centralize customer information and improve customer relationship management through automation, reporting, and communication tools. Research explored CRM history, evolution, business use cases, and common modules.

---

## CRM Product Comparisons

Commercial and open-source CRM systems were compared based on target customers, pricing models, features, scalability, and implementation complexity. Recommendations were developed for both small business and enterprise environments.

---

## Open Source CRM Evaluation

EspoCRM was selected for evaluation using its online demo environment. The evaluation focused on usability, module organization, reporting features, and practical business applications.

---

## CRM Architecture Proposal

A CRM architecture was proposed using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, Bootstrap, PHP, and MySQL. The design included customer management modules, security considerations, and database planning.

## Lessons Learned

This project demonstrated that AI can accelerate technical research and software evaluation while highlighting the importance of validating generated information. Effective prompt engineering significantly improved research quality and reduced research time.
