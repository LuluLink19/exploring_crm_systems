# Part 5 – AI Reflection and Prompt Engineering

## AI Tools Used

The primary AI tool used during this project was ChatGPT. AI was used to assist with research, product comparisons, software architecture ideas, organization of information, and technical explanations.

---

## Best Prompts Used During Research

### Prompt 1

Explain CRM systems to a software engineering student. Include the definition, purpose, history, and evolution of CRM systems.

### Prompt 2

Compare Salesforce, HubSpot CRM, Zoho CRM, Microsoft Dynamics 365 CRM, and Oracle CRM. Include strengths, weaknesses, pricing models, and target customers.

### Prompt 3

Compare open-source CRM systems including SuiteCRM, EspoCRM, Odoo CRM, and Vtiger CRM. Include installation difficulty, community support, and technology stacks.

### Prompt 4

Design a CRM architecture using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, Bootstrap, PHP, and MySQL. Include modules, database tables, and security recommendations.

### Prompt 5

Explain important security considerations when building a CRM system and provide mitigation strategies for common vulnerabilities.

### Prompt 6

Recommend the minimum viable product features for a CRM designed for small businesses.

---

## AI Effectiveness Analysis

### What AI Did Well

AI was effective at quickly explaining unfamiliar concepts, organizing technical information, generating comparison tables, and helping structure software architecture ideas. It significantly reduced research time by providing summarized explanations and examples.

### What AI Struggled With

AI sometimes produced information that was too general or lacked specific details. Product pricing, popularity rankings, and vendor-specific information occasionally required follow-up prompts or additional verification.

### What Information Required Validation

The information that required the most validation included pricing models, feature availability, popularity claims, and installation requirements because these can change frequently over time.

### What Surprised Me

One surprising aspect was how quickly AI could organize large amounts of technical information into structured formats such as tables, architecture proposals, and comparison summaries.

### What Would I Do Differently Next Time

Next time, I would use more targeted prompts earlier in the process and validate product-specific information sooner to reduce revision time later. I would also compare multiple sources earlier instead of waiting until the end of the research process.

### Would I Trust AI For Software Research?

AI is useful for accelerating research and learning unfamiliar topics, but it should not be used as the only source of information. AI-generated content should still be validated using documentation, vendor websites, and additional research sources before making decisions.
